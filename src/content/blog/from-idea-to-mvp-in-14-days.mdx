Every founder knows the feeling: you've got the idea, you've validated the market, and now you need to *build*. But hiring a team takes months. Freelancers are a gamble. And no-code tools hit a wall the moment you need real logic.

That's where we come in.

## The Challenge

Our client, a proptech startup founder, came to us with a clear problem: property managers were drowning in spreadsheets. Tenant communications, maintenance requests, lease tracking — all scattered across email, texts, and Google Sheets.

They needed a centralized platform. Fast.

## Our Approach

### Week 1: Foundation

We started with a 2-hour scope call. By the end of day one, we had:

- A complete data model (tenants, properties, leases, maintenance tickets)
- Authentication with role-based access (owner, manager, tenant)
- A CI/CD pipeline deploying to Vercel on every push

By day 3, the core CRUD operations were live. By day 5, we had a working dashboard with real-time data.

### Week 2: Polish & Launch

Week two was all about the details that make a product feel *real*:

- Stripe billing integration with usage-based pricing
- Automated email notifications for maintenance updates
- A tenant-facing portal for submitting requests
- Mobile-responsive design with dark mode

## The Result

On day 14, the founder sent their first invite to a paying customer. The platform handled 50+ tenants in its first month with zero downtime.

**The 90% principle in action:** We didn't build every feature imaginable. We built the features that mattered *now* and set up the architecture to scale for what comes next.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Scope ruthlessly.** The MVP isn't the product — it's the starting line.
2. **Choose boring technology.** Next.js, PostgreSQL, Stripe — proven tools that let you move fast.
3. **Ship, then iterate.** Real user feedback is worth more than months of planning.

## What's Next

The founder is now onboarding their 10th property management company. The platform has grown from a simple dashboard to a full-featured SaaS product with automated billing, maintenance workflows, and a tenant mobile app.

We continue to support the product with monthly feature sprints, each one guided by real user data and feedback.
